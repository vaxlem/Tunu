meaning of the name

Tunu.

According to the narratives, the name Tunu is an appellation that is coined by these men. Prehku and Lakleh Tay, over centuries ago.



FULL STORY.

One day in a very long time, a man called Papa Preku went to hunt wild animals in the place. He was a hunter. The animals were disturbing on this hill. So Preku was sent by his partner Lakleh to go on the hunting expedition. Upon going, he set his trap by fixing a gun on the pathway where the beasts move on or pass when they are roaming. After setting his gun he would climb a tree and by means of a rope, he would pull the triger of the gun he set on the ground at the animals from the tree top .
He would then complete the killing by firing other riffles and amunitions he had with him at the top of the tree. In no time all the dangerous animals including lions were no longer disturbing, they were cleared off. This bravery amazed all other hunters and the people because they tried and failed and they wonder how he did it. Therefore, they would ask him how he did it.
And proudly, Preku would say (' Afisia la, Etunue wonye.'). To wit, "this place, it is a muzzle of a Gun. When one tries one dies."
This fame naturally resulted in the Famous name Tunu.
(at the barrel and muzzle of a gun.)

The residents of Ada and Anlo enjoy an unusual bond. This connection went a long way toward giving rise to the term Tunu. According to the narratives, the inhabitants of Ada were fishermen. But that's not all; they also farm and trade with the inhabitants of Anlo beyond the Volta River. At one point, several of them did engage in palm wine tapping. This is exactly what occurred. The catchment region was discovered by the leaders of the Klevie Clan, a clan in Anlo. This extended all the way to Fuveme (Azizanu) and all the way to Dzita and beyond.


Overtime, the Klevie members gave portions of the catchment area to the folks from Ada and for some period, these Ada residents planted palm trees on the site and used to tap and brew wine from the palm plantation.
Then all of a sudden , they noticed a theft of their wine and accused the Klevi people of stealing their wine. As a result, they used their deity to cast a spell on the Klevi clan. The matter become hot, so therefore, it took another clan (BATEH CLAN) members/elders to pay on behalf of the Klevi Clan. The Klevie people transferred the land to the Bateh clan as a result of this kindness from the Bateh clan. This occurrence illustrates how the Bateh people control the catchment region. Later, wild animals in the forest caused concern, and when this became significant, the decision was made to eliminate them. So a man named Agbahnyo from the Bateh tribe enlisted the support of valiant warriors to help him exterminate the wild animals. At this point, a hunter from Srogboe-Wuti approached and offered to do the job. LAKLEH TAY EHA was the name of this individual.


Lakleh and the beasts.


Immediately, Lakleh sent his hunting partner in the person of Preku, to the area to undertake the expedition. Preku also agreed and went on this adventure.


Prehku.


When Prehku went to assess the region, he and Lakleh devised a game plan that worked. After their achievement, Agbanyo rewarded them (the hunters) with a share of the land. They relocated to the area because of the body of water nearby in order to fish.
